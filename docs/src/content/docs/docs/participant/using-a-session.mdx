You're here to *use* a workshop — an RStudio or JupyterLab environment that
someone set up for a course or conference. Orchestra runs it for you in the
cloud; there's nothing to install.

## Launch and connect

<Steps>

1. Open the Orchestra dashboard (your instructor will give you the link) and
   sign in.

2. Pick a workshop from the catalog and choose **Launch**. It takes a moment to
   start — the status moves from *Pending* to *Starting* to *Ready*.

3. When it's **Ready**, choose **Open** (or **Connect**) to enter your session
   in a new tab.

</Steps>

## Your session is temporary

Every session has a time limit, shown as a countdown on its card. This keeps the
cluster available for everyone — but it means:

<Aside type="caution" title="Save your work before the timer runs out">
When a session expires (or you end it), everything in it is removed — including
files you created. Download anything you want to keep — notebooks, scripts,
results — **before** it expires. There is no way to recover a session after it's
gone.
</Aside>

## Get more time

If you're running low, use **+1h** on the session card to extend it. You'll see
a warning as the deadline approaches, and again when it's close.

## Something went wrong?

- **Stuck on *Starting* for a while** — some images are large and take a few
  minutes on first launch. If it never reaches *Ready*, tell your instructor.
- **Session disappeared** — it likely expired. Launch a fresh one; remember to
  save your work this time.
- **Can't open it** — your workshop may still be starting, or the session ended.
  Check the status on the dashboard.
